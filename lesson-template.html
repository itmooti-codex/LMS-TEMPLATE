Dive Into the Lesson Notes

Strong introductions signal confidence and clarity. Start by centering your reader with a vivid moment or bold statement, then anchor expectations with a clear promise.

Remember: Your goal is to answer a reader’s silent question of “why should I care?” within the first three sentences. Use sensory details, highlight stakes, and hint at the transformation to come.

“An introduction is your handshake with the reader—firm, warm, and purposeful.”
  • Draft multiple openers before committing to one.
  • Read your introduction aloud to check rhythm and flow.
  • Invite a peer to reflect what they expect from the rest of the piece.

Close your introduction with a sentence that transitions seamlessly into the body. This bridge prepares readers for what comes next and reinforces your central message.
